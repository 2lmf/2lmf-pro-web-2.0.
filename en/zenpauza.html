The Peace Industry: Why do we pay 70 € a year to listen to rain?

PUBLISHED: MARCH 7, 2026 | AUTHOR: SHARPSHARK DIGITAL

Have you ever tried to meditate, study, or simply fall asleep with one of the popular relaxation apps? Open the app, set the rain sound, close your eyes and just as you start to drift off... BAM! A loud ad for a mobile game. Want to remove ads and listen for longer than 2 hours? No problem, that will cost you 6.99 € a month.

Today we pay a "peace tax". Focus apps, breathing exercises, and habit trackers have become a luxury. Why do they charge us for access to something as simple as deep breathing or generating white noise? And more importantly – what do they actually do with your habit data?

The Problem: Fragmentation and hidden costs

To have the "ideal" set of productivity tools today, your phone looks like this:

  • One app for white noise (which eats battery because it downloads huge audio files).
  • Another app for breathing exercises (which forces you to create an account).
  • A third app for habit tracking (which saves your data on who-knows-which servers).

All of that together costs money, slows down your phone, and sends you 15 notifications a day.

How is it possible that this is free and of such high quality?

There's no catch, just smarter technology. Big apps have to charge a subscription because they use expensive servers to send you heavy audio files. We programmed Zen Pauza so that your own phone generates the sound itself using the built-in Web Audio API.

Since we don't have the costs of expensive servers and we don't resell your data – we have no reason to charge you a subscription!
