How much does a modern panel fence cost in 2026? (Guide for smart procurement)

PUBLISHED: FEBRUARY 24, 2026 | AUTHOR: 2LMF PRO

Panel fences have become an absolute standard of modern construction in Croatia. From private courtyards to commercial buildings, their clean design, durability and simple installation make them the first choice for investors. However, when planning a budget, many rely on general prices "per square meter" from various catalogs, and the real amount at the end of the cycle is often different.

Here's what to watch out for when purchasing materials and how to avoid the stress of manual offer creation.

The challenge of assembly elements (What makes a fence?)

Unlike ordinary wire mesh, a panel fence is a modular system. The price of the panel itself is only one part of the equation. To get a functional fence, technical elements must be strictly harmonized, and this is often the "math" where many get stuck:

  • Posts: Their height directly depends on your terrain. Do you need longer posts for direct concreting into the ground, or shorter posts with anchor plates for installation on an existing concrete parapet?
  • Connectors and clamps: They make joints firm and secure. Their required number varies depending on the number of posts and the height of the fence.
  • Pedestrian gates: Integrating a gate into the system requires adjusting adjacent panels and additional support posts.

When buying "piece by piece" traditionally, people often buy too many elements (which is wasted money) or too few (which stops the craftsmen's work).

2D or 3D Panels – What to choose?

When defining technical details, you face a visual dilemma about the shape of the panel itself. It's not all just about aesthetics:

  • 3D Panel Fences: Recognizable by their "V" crimps. These crimps give extraordinary static strength to a relatively thin wire. This is proven to be the most popular and affordable option for fencing private yards and larger building plots.
  • 2D Panel Fences: "Heavy artillery". They don't have crimps, they are completely flat, but they come with double horizontal wire (so-called 6/5/6 mm system). They are extremely strong and resistant to bending, therefore ideal for places with higher mechanical loads (like sports fields or locations with strong wind gusts). Due to the larger amount of steel, the initial investment in 2D panels is slightly higher than the 3D version.
